Notes & methodology

  • How SPMO churns: the S&P 500 Momentum Index rebalances twice a year, effective after the close of the third Friday of March and September (reference data as of end-February / end-August). It scores all S&P 500 names on 12-month risk-adjusted momentum, keeps the top ~100, and weights by market cap × momentum score with a ~10% issuer cap. Between rebalances the fund does not trade — weights drift purely with relative price moves.
  • What this page shows: the drift of the current basket since the last effective date. Baseline weights are reconstructed from the fund's current share counts × closing prices on the rebalance date (valid because creations/redemptions scale all positions pro-rata). Cross-checked against the fund's published weights to ~0.1–0.2pp.
  • Why you can't buy SPMO directly: as a UK (Ltd/retail) account, IBKR blocks US-domiciled ETFs under the PRIIPs/KID rules. Individual US stocks are unaffected — hence replicating the basket manually.
  • Entering mid-cycle: buying at current weights replicates what SPMO actually holds today. Buying at rebalance-day weights would recreate a stale book (and implicitly bet against the momentum that has since played out). Note that at the next churn (~) roughly 40–55% of names historically turn over — expect to re-trade the pie then.
  • Caveats: prices are split/dividend-adjusted closes (Yahoo Finance); dividend adjustment shifts past weights by at most a few hundredths of a pp. Small residual differences vs Invesco's published weights come from cash/securities-lending lines and pricing-time differences. Names removed mid-cycle (e.g. M&A) would not appear here.
  • This is a monitoring tool, not investment advice. Momentum strategies are prone to sharp crashes at market turns.
